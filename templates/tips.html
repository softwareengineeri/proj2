Tire Safety Tips

1. Regularly Check Tire Pressure

Ensure your tires are properly inflated to the recommended pressure. Under-inflated or over-inflated tires can affect fuel efficiency and tire wear.

2. Inspect Tread Depth

Check the tread depth of your tires regularly. Worn-out tread can reduce traction, especially in wet or snowy conditions.

3. Rotate Tires

Rotate your tires regularly to promote even wear. This can extend the lifespan of your tires and improve vehicle performance.

4. Avoid Overloading

Avoid overloading your vehicle, as it can put excessive strain on your tires and lead to premature wear.

5. Check for Signs of Damage

Inspect your tires for any signs of damage, such as cuts, bulges, or cracks. Replace damaged tires immediately to ensure safety on the road.
